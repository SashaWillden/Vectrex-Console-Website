Vectrex Console:

The Vectrex console is one of the second generation consoles that was released in 1982 that had a bootup screen in the feature system, but the console was short-lived in the entry of the home console market by General Consumer Electronics (GCE). Until this day, the generation who played it has never forgotten the console of how unique it is and the console came with an integrated vector monitor, which used plastic screen overlays to emulate colours, a built-in controller and a popular built-in game called Minestorm.

In the 1980s, vector graphic video games were very popular in arcades. Those who know about the Vectrex console would most likely understand their childhood experience of the console and how expensive the console was on the release date.

Console Games:

The Vectrex was not only known for its unique design which consisted of a built in screen and a 3D headgear, but it came with loads of amazing games that many of us still remember and cherish today. This included games such as Minestorm, Armor Attack, Pole Position, and Berzerk. Many of which were originally arcade games ported to the Vectrex so we could play in the comfort of our living rooms.
